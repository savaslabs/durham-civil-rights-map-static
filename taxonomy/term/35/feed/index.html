But the publication quickly became more robust, growing to include essays, poetry, local and regional news, announcements, book and movie reviews, and classified ads.</p> <p>While Feminary had always focused on the subject of lesbian-feminism and issues surrounding lesbians within the feminist movement, in 1978 Feminary issued a statement that it would focus exclusively on lesbian-feminist subjects. “As Southerners, as lesbians, and as women,” the statement read, “we need to explore with others how our lives fit into a region about which we have great ambivalences—to share our anger and our love.” Feminary—from then on called Feminary: A Feminist Journal for the South, Emphasizing the Lesbian Vision—did just that. The journal published seasonally, producing poetry, personal essays, art, interviews, news, and book reviews of lesbian women in the South. In the words of Mab Segrest, one of Feminary’s core writers, Feminary’s contributors understood their work as “creating lesbian consciousness and lesbian community…We were a crucible for sorting through questions of identity.”</p> <p>Feminary published its last issue in the spring of 1982, at which point many of its core writers were leaving North Carolina. The group attempted to move the journal to San Francisco under new management, but only two issues were ever published after the move. </p> <p>For more information:</p> <p><a href="http://outhistory.org/exhibits/show/nc-lgbt/periodicals/Feminary">OutHistory.Org</a></p> <p><a href="http://sites.duke.edu/docst110s_01_s2011_bec15/print-culture/feminary-newsletter/"><strong>1970s North Carolina Feminisms (Duke Website)</strong></a></p></div> <div class="field field--name-field-photos field--type-image field--label-visually_hidden"> <div class="field__label visually-hidden">Photos</div> <div class="field__items"> <div class="field__item"> <img src="http://samdufrin.com/sites/default/files/place_photos/Feminary1.jpg" width="600" height="600" alt="Journal Cover" typeof="foaf:Image" /></div> <div class="field__item"> <img src="http://samdufrin.com/sites/default/files/place_photos/Feminary2.jpg" width="1281" height="804" alt="Text from the Journal" typeof="foaf:Image" /></div> </div> </div> <div class="field field--name-field-date field--type-datetime field--label-above"> <div class="field__label">Date</div> <div class="field__items"> <div class="field__item"><time datetime="1969-08-11T12:00:00Z" class="datetime">Mon, 08/11/1969 - Durham YWCA and Women's Center http://samdufrin.com/places/21-fighting-for-womens-rights-durham-ywca-and-womens-center <span class="field field--name-title field--type-string field--label-hidden">Fighting for Women's Rights - Durham YWCA and Women's Center</span> <span class="field field--name-uid field--type-entity-reference field--label-hidden"><span lang="" about="http://samdufrin.com/user/233" typeof="schema:Person" property="schema:name" datatype="" xml:lang="">balau</span></span> <span class="field field--name-created field--type-created field--label-hidden">Tue, 04/19/2016 - 16:21</span> <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-body field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p>A safe space to gather, a safe place to unite. A place to be proud of your identity.  In 1971, eight years after Betty Freidan’s publication <em>The Feminine Mystique</em> provided a manifesto for the movement of second-wave feminism in the United States, the Durham YWCA opened its internally-sponsored Women’s Center. The program was first led by YWCA program committee chair Muriel Smith, with the original intention of simply creating a Women’s Library focused upon literature on women’s rights and history. However, once greater need for a center of feminist action was realized, Smith’s vision for the library quickly expanded to become much, much more. The project’s scope widened to include not only a library, but also a meeting space as a centralized location for the other women’s interest groups present in Durham.</p> <p>The center rapidly became <em>the</em> hub of feminist activity for women from all walks of life. In the words of historian Dr. Melissa Estes, the Women’s Center allowed the YWCA to “serve as the public face of feminism in the city.” After the center’s creation, it sparked a wave of other programs that established their own roots in the triangle community with the support of the Women’s Center, such as the Durham Women’s Health Collective, Triangle Area Lesbian Feminists, the Women’s Health Teaching Group, and even included a day care for working women and a rape crisis hotline.</p> <p>Throughout 1972 and 1973, the Women’s Center held their series of highly successful public workshops on a variety of topics and feminist issues that affected women’s daily lives. These included everything from routine car repair and maintenance, to Black women and Feminism, to body image and healthy relationships. The extremely active League of Women’s Voters also routinely offered workshops and panels on the Equal Rights Amendment.</p> <p>The Women’s center created a “safe space” for women to be a part of a large and supportive community, crucial in a time when feminism operated on ideals that were still considered controversial and radical in the eyes of many who adhered to traditional conservative stereotypes about the female identity. </p> <p>For more information: <a href="http://www.torightthesewrongs.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/05/Rankin-Sarah-The-Power-of-Money.pdf">http://muse.jhu.edu/article/370522, http://www.opendurham.org/buildings/ywca</a></p></div> <div class="field field--name-field-photos field--type-image field--label-visually_hidden"> <div class="field__label visually-hidden">Photos</div> <div class="field__items"> <div class="field__item"> <img src="http://samdufrin.com/sites/default/files/place_photos/YWCA%20image%201.jpg" width="320" height="240" alt="YWCA Durham in the 1950s" typeof="foaf:Image" /></div> </div> </div> <div class="field field--name-field-date field--type-datetime field--label-above"> <div class="field__label">Date</div> <div class="field__items"> <div class="field__item"><time datetime="1971-06-01T12:00:00Z" class="datetime">Tue, 06/01/1971 -
