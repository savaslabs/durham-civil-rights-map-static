But the publication quickly became more robust, growing to include essays, poetry, local and regional news, announcements, book and movie reviews, and classified ads.</p> <p>While Feminary had always focused on the subject of lesbian-feminism and issues surrounding lesbians within the feminist movement, in 1978 Feminary issued a statement that it would focus exclusively on lesbian-feminist subjects. “As Southerners, as lesbians, and as women,” the statement read, “we need to explore with others how our lives fit into a region about which we have great ambivalences—to share our anger and our love.” Feminary—from then on called Feminary: A Feminist Journal for the South, Emphasizing the Lesbian Vision—did just that. The journal published seasonally, producing poetry, personal essays, art, interviews, news, and book reviews of lesbian women in the South. In the words of Mab Segrest, one of Feminary’s core writers, Feminary’s contributors understood their work as “creating lesbian consciousness and lesbian community…We were a crucible for sorting through questions of identity.”</p> <p>Feminary published its last issue in the spring of 1982, at which point many of its core writers were leaving North Carolina. The group attempted to move the journal to San Francisco under new management, but only two issues were ever published after the move. </p> <p>For more information:</p> <p><a href="http://outhistory.org/exhibits/show/nc-lgbt/periodicals/Feminary">OutHistory.Org</a></p> <p><a href="http://sites.duke.edu/docst110s_01_s2011_bec15/print-culture/feminary-newsletter/"><strong>1970s North Carolina Feminisms (Duke Website)</strong></a></p></div> <div class="field field--name-field-photos field--type-image field--label-visually_hidden"> <div class="field__label visually-hidden">Photos</div> <div class="field__items"> <div class="field__item"> <img src="http://savaslabs.github.io/sites/default/files/place_photos/Feminary1.jpg" width="600" height="600" alt="Journal Cover" typeof="foaf:Image" /> </div> <div class="field__item"> <img src="http://savaslabs.github.io/sites/default/files/place_photos/Feminary2.jpg" width="1281" height="804" alt="Text from the Journal" typeof="foaf:Image" /> </div> </div> </div> <div class="field field--name-field-date field--type-datetime field--label-above"> <div class="field__label">Date</div> <div class="field__items"> <div class="field__item"><time datetime="1969-08-11T12:00:00Z" class="datetime">Mon, 08/11/1969 -
